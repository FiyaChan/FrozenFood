Tips & Tricks: Cooking All Types of Frozen Food

A quick reference guide for three main cooking methods — Fried / Deep-fried, Steamed, and Baked.

Fried & Deep-fried

Crispy outside, evenly cooked inside. Examples: nuggets, fries, spring rolls.

  • Do not defrost — fry straight from frozen for best crispiness.
  • Oil temperature: 170–180°C (340–355°F). Fry in small batches to keep oil hot.
  • Drain on a rack, not paper towels, to prevent sogginess.
  • Extra crisp trick: dust lightly with cornstarch or breadcrumbs before frying.

Steamed

Soft and juicy results. Examples: dim sum, buns, dumplings, fish cakes.

  • Let thaw for about 5–10 minutes before steaming (not fully defrosted).
  • Use boiling water before placing food; wrap the steamer lid with a cloth to avoid water dripping.
  • Use banana leaf or baking paper to prevent sticking.
  • Approx times: dumplings 5–7 min, buns 10–15 min, fishcakes 8–10 min.

Baked (Oven)

Golden crisp outside, moist inside. Examples: pastries, mini pizzas, frozen lasagna, wedges.

  • Preheat oven 5–10 minutes. Use a rack or perforated tray for better air flow.
  • Brush a bit of oil or butter on pastries or breaded foods for a golden finish.
  • Cover cheese-topped dishes with foil for the first half to avoid burning.
  • For an “air fryer” effect: 200°C (390°F), fan mode, 12–15 min (approx.).

General Tips

  • Preheat your cooking tool (air fryer, oven, pan) for 3–5 minutes before cooking.
  • Do not overcrowd — give enough space for heat to circulate evenly.
  • Shake halfway if using an air fryer for even browning.
  • Store in airtight ziplock bags, squeeze out air to avoid frost buildup.

Quick Reference (Time & Temperature — Approx.)

// Fried: 170–180°C (340–355°F), 5–12 min depending on size
// Steamed: dumpling 5–7 min | bun 10–15 min
// Baked: 180–200°C (355–390°F), 10–20 min depending on item
